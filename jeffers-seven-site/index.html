Practice

About

Jeffers Seven Consulting is an independent advisory practice serving hardware deep tech companies — SBIR Phase II winners, growth-stage manufacturers, and venture investors in the space.

Principal Nick Jeffers brings twenty-five years of advanced manufacturing engineering experience at a major aerospace OEM, an M.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering, and an active independent research portfolio in theoretical physics.

The practice provides engineering-grounded advisory — not generic consulting. The principal has actually run manufacturing organizations at scale, navigated DoD acquisition cycles, and built production systems for safety-critical applications.

Services

What we do

R&D Tax Credit Studies

Formal IRC §41 R&D credit studies for hardware deep tech companies. Specializing in SBIR-aware methodology where federal funding and company-funded R&D require careful allocation. Engineer-led qualifying activity identification that generalist firms miss.

SBIR Phase II Support

Proposal writing, technical narrative development, commercialization planning, and program management for SBIR Phase II awards. Win-stage, execution-stage, and Phase III transition support.

Strategic Manufacturing Advisory

Manufacturing scale-up advisory from prototype to production. Supply chain strategy. Prime supplier qualification roadmaps. Organizational design for hardware engineering teams.

Tech Due Diligence for Investors

Independent technical assessment of hardware deep tech companies for venture investors. Manufacturing readiness, supply chain risk, IP position, commercialization plausibility.

Scope

What we don’t do

Clarity about scope is part of the service.

  • We don’t prepare or file tax returns. We provide R&D Credit Studies; the client’s CPA files the return.
  • We don’t represent clients before the IRS or any taxing authority. We are not enrolled agents.
  • We don’t provide legal advice. We are not attorneys.
  • We don’t engage where current employer industry conflict exists (aerospace propulsion competitive territory).
